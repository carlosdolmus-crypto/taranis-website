The Big Picture

Consolidating a Historic Mineral Province

Taranis owns 100% of the Silver Cup Mining District. We have consolidated multiple historic high-grade mines under a single, systematic geological model.

Flagship Resource

The Thor Deposit

The cornerstone of Taranis' value. A highly concentrated polymetallic deposit stretching over 2 km on the surface, already containing substantial indicated silver, gold, zinc, lead, and copper resources.

  • Geology: Epithermal/exhalative massive sulfide sheet
  • Status: 250+ drill holes, resource defined, permitted for bulk sample
  • Key Target: Open at depth and along strike
High-Priority Target

The Borr Target

Located 1.5 km northwest of Thor. Borr represents a major geophysical anomaly mirroring the signature of Thor, indicating a potential repeat of the massive sulfide mineralization.

  • Geology: Intense alteration zone with strong silver-lead surface anomalies
  • Status: Geophysics completed, targeted for exploratory drilling
  • Key Target: Strike continuity of the main Silver Cup fault system
Exploration Target

The Nortran Zone

A highly complex structural block displaying intense shearing and mineralized outcrops. Historic records show high-grade silver was hand-mined here at the turn of the 20th century.

  • Geology: High-grade polymetallic veins in quartz-carbonate shear zones
  • Status: Detailed mapping & geological modeling ongoing
  • Key Target: Unlocking structural offsets that hold high-grade shoots
District Extension

The Ram Target

Marking the northern frontier of our consolidated claim block. Ram lies on the continuation of the 14 km mineralized corridor, demonstrating the immense regional scale of the plumbing system.

  • Geology: Outcropping volcanic-hosted massive sulfide (VMS) indicators
  • Status: Geophysics and soil geochemistry completed
  • Key Target: Massive regional extension of the district footprint
Exploration Target

One O'Clock

A massive, untested alteration anomaly on the eastern flank. Represents a major structural block fed directly by the underlying engine.

  • Geology: Intense alteration zone
  • Status: Untested anomaly
  • Key Target: Intrusive contact zone
The Magmatic Engine

I-1 Intrusive

The ultimate geological prize. Magnetotelluric (MT) surveys have identified a massive, deep-seated conductive body directly beneath the Thor deposit. This intrusive is the likely hydrothermal engine that fed all mineralized zones in the district.

  • Geology: Deep porphyry/intrusive complex (1-2 km depth)
  • Status: Confirmed by magnetotellurics and deep drilling indicators
  • Key Target: Large-scale copper-gold-silver porphyry system

The Thor Deposit

At the surface, the high-grade Thor deposit provides a massive base of mineral wealth. Easily accessible, highly concentrated, and thoroughly drilled, Thor acts as the foundation of investor confidence. But the geological architecture points to something much bigger.

02 / THE SHADOW

The Tusks Alteration Halo

Surrounding and extending far beyond Thor lies the Tusks Alteration Halo. This massive geochemical and mineralogical footprint represents rock that was cooked and chemically altered by superheated hydrothermal fluids—the unmistakable calling card of a giant heat source nearby.

03 / THE ENGINE

The I-1 Intrusive

Directly underneath Thor, geophysics has revealed "The Elephant"—the I-1 Intrusive. This massive, deep-seated magmatic engine acted as the central volcanic pump, forcing millions of tonnes of mineral-rich fluids upward through geological fault zones.
